## Full Findings

**Claim 1: 18.1M Americans identify as digital nomads in 2025, up 147% from 7.3M in 2019**
- Paper says: 18.1M digital nomads in 2025, up 147% from 7.3M in 2019, per MBO Partners 2025 Trends Report
- Found: MBO Partners' 2025 Digital Nomads Trends Report actually reports **18.5 million** Americans as digital nomads in 2025, a **153% increase** since 2019. The 18.1M figure with the 147% increase appears in a Staffing Industry Analysts article referencing an earlier version of the data (likely the 2024 report measuring 2024 numbers). The 7.3M 2019 baseline is consistent across sources. The paper appears to have cited the 2024 data point while labeling it as 2025.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED
- Source: https://www.mbopartners.com/state-of-independence/digital-nomads/

**Claim 2: 102.4% jump in Americans expatriating in Q1 2025 vs Q4 2024; projected 4,936 expatriations in 2025 (highest since 2020)**
- Paper says: 102.4% jump Q1 2025 vs Q4 2024; 1,285 expatriations in Q1; projected 4,936 for full year (highest since 2020), per CS Global Partners
- Found: CS Global Partners reporting confirms "an estimated 1,285 U.S. citizens expatriated in the first quarter of 2025, marking a 102% increase compared with the last quarter of 2024." The projected 4,936 total for 2025 and the reference to 2020 as the previous peak year are consistent with the WebSearch summary of CS Global Partners data. The paper's "102.4%" is a slightly more precise rendering of what sources round to "102%."
- Verdict: CONFIRMED
- Source: https://csglobalpartners.com/news/us-expat-numbers-double-in-2025-as-americans-seek-new-lives-abroad/

**Claim 3: California FTB completed 520 residency audits in 2023, up from 230 in 2019 (126% increase). FTB penalty notices surged 32% in 2025.**
- Paper says: 520 audits in 2023, up 126% from ~230 in 2019; penalty notices surged 32% in 2025, per yourtaxbase.com and corporatedirect.com
- Found: The yourtaxbase.com article confirms "The FTB completed 520 residency audits on out-of-state residents in 2023" and states this is "up 126% from 2019," which implies ~230 audits in 2019. However, the claim that "FTB penalty notices surged 32% in 2025" was **not found** on either yourtaxbase.com or in any other search result. This specific statistic could not be independently verified from web sources.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED
- Source: https://yourtaxbase.com/blog/terminate-california-residency-avoid-ftb-audit

**Claim 4: FEIE exclusion: $130,000 for 2025, $132,900 for 2026**
- Paper says: FEIE maximum exclusion is $130,000 for tax year 2025 and $132,900 for tax year 2026, per IRS via multiple guides
- Found: Multiple authoritative tax sources confirm the FEIE is $130,000 for tax year 2025 and $132,900 for tax year 2026, adjusted annually for inflation under IRC Section 911. The IRS page itself lists historical figures but the amounts are confirmed by Taxes for Expats, Greenback Tax Services, and others citing official IRS guidance.
- Verdict: CONFIRMED
- Source: https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/tax-saving-strategies/foreign-earned-income-exclusion.html
