# Research Plan: Nomad Visa in US — User Pain Points for Micro-SaaS

## Tier: Medium
- Searches: 15–30 (soft limit)
- Fetches: 30–70 (soft limit)
- Subagents: 4

## Strategy
Breadth-first: scan forums, Reddit, reviews, competitor gaps, and professional advice sites to surface recurring complaints and unmet needs. Then depth on the most promising pain clusters.

## Research Tracks

### Track 1: Forum & Reddit Pain Points
**Objective:** Mine Reddit (r/digitalnomad, r/tax, r/expats, r/usvisa), Nomad List forums, and Facebook group discussions for recurring complaints about US nomad visa/tax/residency.
**Output:** `track-1-forums.md`
**Budget:** ~5 searches, ~10 fetches

### Track 2: Existing Tool Gaps & Negative Reviews
**Objective:** Analyze reviews and complaints about existing tools (TaxBird, TaxDay, Domicile365, SavvyNomad, NomadTax, etc.) — what are users unhappy about? What's missing?
**Output:** `track-2-tool-gaps.md`
**Budget:** ~4 searches, ~10 fetches

### Track 3: Tax & Legal Compliance Pain Points
**Objective:** Research specific pain points around multi-state tax filing, nexus rules, state residency thresholds, estimated tax payments, and FBAR/FATCA compliance for nomads.
**Output:** `track-3-tax-legal.md`
**Budget:** ~4 searches, ~10 fetches

### Track 4: Logistics & Lifestyle Pain Points
**Objective:** Research pain points around mail forwarding, domicile setup, healthcare/insurance, banking, vehicle registration, and other practical logistics that nomads in the US struggle with.
**Output:** `track-4-logistics.md`
**Budget:** ~4 searches, ~10 fetches

## Subagent Boundaries
- Track 1 focuses on user voice (forums, social media)
- Track 2 focuses on competitor product gaps (reviews, feature comparisons)
- Track 3 focuses on tax/legal complexity
- Track 4 focuses on non-tax logistics
- Overlap: if a forum post mentions a specific tool gap, Track 1 notes it but Track 2 goes deeper on the tool
