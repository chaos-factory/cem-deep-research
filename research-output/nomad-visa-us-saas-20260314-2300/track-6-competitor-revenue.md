- **TaxBird** (residency day tracker) charges $39.99/year or $4.99/month; 232 App Store ratings suggest ~5K-15K total downloads. Small bootstrapped product. ([App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/taxbird-residency-tracker/id1238166926))

- **Nomad Tax** offers free consultations and CPA services for digital nomads; pricing not publicly listed (likely $500-$1,000+ per engagement based on comparable services). ([nomadtax.io](https://www.nomadtax.io/))
- **Business model pattern**: Tax prep services cluster around $175-$800/return (one-time, seasonal). Mail forwarding is $110-$250/year subscription. Day-tracking apps are $30-$120/year. Bundled domicile+tax services (SavvyNomad model) command $660+/year recurring -- highest LTV potential among non-insurance players.

## Full Findings

- **Valuation**: $195M post-money (2022)

- **Users**: 25,000+ protected individuals as of early 2023

### Business Model Effectiveness
1. **Insurance (SafetyWing)**: Highest revenue, true recurring, large TAM. Only VC-scale company in the group.
2. **Bundled subscription (SavvyNomad)**: Highest non-insurance LTV potential. Recurring revenue from domicile + mail creates lock-in, with tax filing as seasonal upsell.
3. **Self-filing software (MyExpatTaxes)**: Better unit economics than CPA model but seasonal/one-time revenue. Low marginal cost per user.
4. **Full-service CPA (Bright!Tax, Greenback)**: High ARPU but labor-intensive, hard to scale. Seasonal revenue concentration.
5. **Mail forwarding (Escapees, America's Mailbox)**: Low ARPU but very sticky (address-of-record creates switching costs). Mature, commodity market.
6. **Day-tracking apps (TaxBird, Flamingo, Domicile365)**: Lowest ARPU, smallest market. Feature rather than standalone business; likely acquisition targets or loss-leaders.
