# Track 2: US Nomad Tax Compliance — Pain Points, Penalties & Costs

## Summary

- **FBAR non-willful penalty**: up to $16,536 per report/year (post-Bittner v. US); willful penalty: greater of $165,353 or 50% of account balance; criminal: up to $500K and/or 10 years ([sdocpa.com](https://www.sdocpa.com/fbar-penalties-relief/), [taxesforexpats.com](https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/fbar-fatca/fbar-penalties.html))
- **FEIE exclusion**: $130,000 for 2025, $132,900 for 2026; housing limitation ~30% of FEIE ($39,000/$39,870) ([IRS](https://www.irs.gov/individuals/international-taxpayers/foreign-earned-income-exclusion), [greenbacktaxservices.com](https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/blog/irs-tax-inflation-adjustments-2026/))
- **Self-employment tax of 15.3% is NOT reduced by FEIE** — nomad freelancers owe SE tax on all net earnings even if income-tax-free via exclusion; SS wage cap $176,100 (2025), $184,500 (2026) ([brighttax.com](https://brighttax.com/blog/digital-nomad-taxes-a-complete-guide/), [greenbacktaxservices.com](https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/knowledge-center/digital-nomad-taxes/))
- **Form 5471 penalty**: $10,000 per foreign corporation per year for non-filing; additional $10,000/30-day period after IRS notice (capped at $50K additional); also triggers 10% reduction in foreign tax credits ([IRS instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i5471))
- **Form 5471 has 5 filer categories** — any US person with 10%+ ownership or control of a foreign corp must file; common trap for nomads who incorporate abroad ([IRS instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i5471))
- **California is the stickiest state**: no simple day-count rule; uses "closest connections" test; 546-day safe harbor requires employment contract + ≤45 days/yr in CA; top rate 13.3%; equity compensation taxed based on where work was performed ([aspire-planning.com](https://aspire-planning.com/blog/2025/2/28/leaving-california-for-a-career-move-or-the-digital-nomad-lifestyle-you-might-still-be-on-the-hook-for-state-taxes))
- **New York**: 184-day + permanent abode rule; state rate up to 10.9%, NYC adds up to 3.876%; combined top rate ~14.8% ([brighttax.com](https://brighttax.com/blog/change-state-tax-residency/))
- **Virginia** identified as sticky state but with less documented enforcement than CA/NY ([brighttax.com](https://brighttax.com/blog/change-state-tax-residency/))
- **Greenback pricing**: federal expat return from $530; catch-up filing (3 years returns + 6 years FBARs + Streamlined) $1,600 ([greenbacktaxservices.com](https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/blog/us-expat-tax-calculator/))
- **General CPA expat costs**: $450–$1,200/yr for standard federal; complex filings with Form 5471 can exceed $3,000; NSA average CPA rate $180/hr, expat returns take 15–20+ hours ([greenbacktaxservices.com](https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/blog/us-expat-tax-calculator/))
- **Streamlined filing programs**: 0% offshore penalty for foreign residents, 5% of highest balance for US residents — critical relief valve but requires certification of non-willfulness ([taxesforexpats.com](https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/fbar-fatca/fbar-penalties.html))
- **Bittner v. US (2023)** fundamentally changed FBAR math: penalties now per-form, not per-account — a taxpayer with 15 accounts over 4 years faces max ~$66K non-willful penalty instead of 60× individual penalties ([taxesforexpats.com](https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/fbar-fatca/fbar-penalties.html))

## Full Findings

### 1. FBAR (FinCEN Form 114) Penalties

**Filing threshold**: $10,000 aggregate balance across all foreign accounts at any point during the year.

**Non-willful civil penalty (2026)**: Up to $16,536 per annual report. Post-Bittner v. United States (Supreme Court, 2023), this applies per form filed, not per account. Before Bittner, the IRS assessed per-account penalties, which could multiply into hundreds of thousands for taxpayers with multiple accounts.

**Willful civil penalty (2026)**: Greater of $165,353 or 50% of the unreported account balance. "Willful" includes both intentional violations and willful blindness (deliberately avoiding learning about the requirement).

**Criminal penalties**:
- General: up to $250,000 fine and/or 5 years imprisonment
- Aggravated (>$100K in 12 months): up to $500,000 and/or 10 years
- Filing a false FinCEN 114: up to $10,000 and/or 5 years

**Assessment period**: IRS has 6 years from FBAR due date to assess penalties.

**Relief options**:
- Delinquent FBAR Submission Procedures: no penalty if no income tax understatement and not under audit
- Streamlined Foreign Offshore Procedures: 0% penalty for qualifying foreign residents
- Streamlined Domestic Offshore Procedures: 5% of highest unreported balance for US residents

Sources: [SDO CPA](https://www.sdocpa.com/fbar-penalties-relief/), [TaxesForExpats](https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/fbar-fatca/fbar-penalties.html), [Bright!Tax](https://brighttax.com/blog/fbar-penalties-explained/), [Greenback](https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/knowledge-center/fbar-penalties/)

### 2. Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE)

| Tax Year | FEIE Amount | Housing Limitation (30%) |
|-----------|------------|-------------------------|
| 2025 | $130,000 | $39,000 |
| 2026 | $132,900 | $39,870 |

**Qualification**: Either Bona Fide Residence test (full tax year in foreign country as resident) or Physical Presence test (330 full days in foreign country in any 12-month period).

**Per-person limit**: Each qualifying spouse can claim their own exclusion — married couples can exclude up to $260,000 (2025) or $265,800 (2026).

**Critical limitation**: FEIE eliminates federal income tax but does NOT reduce self-employment tax. This is the single biggest surprise for nomad freelancers.

Sources: [IRS](https://www.irs.gov/individuals/international-taxpayers/foreign-earned-income-exclusion), [Greenback](https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/blog/irs-tax-inflation-adjustments-2026/), [TaxesForExpats](https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/tax-saving-strategies/foreign-earned-income-exclusion.html)

### 3. Self-Employment Tax for Nomads

**Rate**: 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare) on net self-employment earnings of $400+.

**Social Security wage base**: $176,100 (2025), $184,500 (2026). Medicare has no cap.

**Additional Medicare tax**: 0.9% on earnings above $200K (single) / $250K (MFJ).

**Key pain point**: FEIE does not offset SE tax. A freelancer earning $130,000 abroad pays $0 in federal income tax (via FEIE) but still owes ~$18,400 in SE tax (on 92.35% of net earnings × 15.3%).

**Totalization agreements**: 30 countries have agreements with the US that can eliminate double social security taxation. However, many popular nomad destinations (Thailand, Portugal, Bali/Indonesia, Mexico, Colombia) do NOT have totalization agreements.

Sources: [Bright!Tax](https://brighttax.com/blog/digital-nomad-taxes-a-complete-guide/), [Greenback](https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/knowledge-center/digital-nomad-taxes/), [MyExpatTaxes](https://www.myexpattaxes.com/expat-tax-tips/self-employment/self-employment-taxes-americans-abroad-expat/)

### 4. Form 5471 (Foreign Corporation Reporting)

**Who must file**: US persons in 5 categories:
1. Category 1: Shareholders of Section 965 specified foreign corporations
2. Category 2: Officers/directors when US person acquires 10%+ stock
3. Category 3: Persons acquiring or disposing of 10%+ ownership
4. Category 4: US persons with majority control of foreign corporation
5. Category 5: US shareholders of controlled foreign corporations (CFCs)

**Nomad relevance**: Any digital nomad who incorporates abroad (common in Estonia e-Residency, UK Ltd, Singapore, etc.) and owns 10%+ triggers Form 5471 filing.

**Penalties**:
- $10,000 per foreign corporation per annual accounting period for failure to file
- Additional $10,000 per 30-day period after 90 days of IRS notice (capped at $50,000 additional per failure)
- Maximum total penalty: $60,000 per corporation per year ($10K initial + $50K continuation)
- Reduction of foreign tax credits by 10%, plus additional 5% per 3-month period of continued non-filing

**Time to complete**: 10–20 hours for simple situations; 40+ hours for complex CFCs with multiple shareholders. Professional preparation commonly costs $1,500–$5,000+ per form.

**Recent development**: Tax Court has ruled the IRS cannot directly assess Form 5471 penalties as assessable penalties — the IRS must refer collection to the Department of Justice for litigation. This creates enforcement uncertainty but does not eliminate the penalty risk.

Sources: [IRS Instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i5471), [Greenback](https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/knowledge-center/form-5471/), [Golding & Golding](https://www.goldinglawyers.com/irs-form-5471-penalty-failure-to-file/), [Loeb & Loeb](https://www.loeb.com/en/insights/publications/2023/04/tax-court-rules-irs-cannot-assess-or-collect-penalties-for-failure-to-file-form-5471)

### 5. State Tax Severance Difficulty

#### California (Top rate: 13.3%)
- **No simple day-count rule** — uses subjective "closest connections" test
- **Safe harbor**: 546-day employment contract abroad, with ≤45 days/year in CA
- **Factors tested**: primary residence, business ties, family connections, time in state, financial accounts, voting registration, professional licenses
- **Community property trap**: If one spouse remains CA resident, they may need to report half of the other spouse's income
- **Equity compensation**: Taxed based on where work was performed, not where you live when you receive it
- **Capital gains**: Source-based — gains from CA business/property remain taxable regardless of residency
- **Real estate withholding**: 3.33% of gross sale price
- **Recommended severance steps**: New domicile, new driver's license, voter registration change, FTB Form 3533, close CA bank accounts, avoid returning for extended periods

#### New York (State rate up to 10.9%, NYC adds up to 3.876%)
- **Day rule**: 184+ days in NY with a permanent abode = statutory resident
- **Domicile**: Separate from statutory residency — can be taxed even with fewer than 184 days if NY is deemed your domicile
- **Combined top rate**: ~14.8% (state + city) for NYC residents
- **548-day foreign safe harbor** available but requires strict spousal compliance
- **Severance steps**: File IT-203 for mid-year moves, withdraw children from NY schools, designate business successors

#### Virginia
- Identified as "sticky state" by multiple sources but less aggressive enforcement than CA/NY
- Specifically mentioned alongside CA, NM, SC as states that pursue expat taxation

Sources: [Aspire Planning](https://aspire-planning.com/blog/2025/2/28/leaving-california-for-a-career-move-or-the-digital-nomad-lifestyle-you-might-still-be-on-the-hook-for-state-taxes), [Bright!Tax](https://brighttax.com/blog/change-state-tax-residency/), [TaxesForExpats](https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/expat-tax-rules/digital-nomad-taxes.html)

### 7. IRS Enforcement & Compliance Data

**FBAR filings**: FinCEN does not regularly publish granular filing statistics. The IRS has 6 years to assess penalties from the due date.

**Bittner impact**: The 2023 Supreme Court decision significantly reduced non-willful penalty exposure. Example: 15 accounts × 4 years = previously up to $600,000+ in penalties; now capped at ~$66,144 (4 × $16,536).

**Form 5471 enforcement uncertainty**: The Tax Court's ruling that the IRS cannot directly assess penalties (must go through DOJ litigation) has created enforcement ambiguity. The IRS is actively pursuing legislative fixes.

**Streamlined program uptake**: The IRS has processed tens of thousands of streamlined submissions since 2014, indicating widespread non-compliance discovery. The 0% penalty for foreign residents makes this an attractive remediation path.
