# Track 4: Market Size — US Digital Nomad Micro-SaaS Tools

## Summary

- **18.5 million Americans** are digital nomads in 2025, up 153% since 2019 (2.2% YoY growth), per [MBO Partners 2025 report](https://www.mbopartners.com/state-of-independence/digital-nomads/)
- **5.5 million US citizens live abroad** (excluding military), per [AARO 2024 estimate](https://aaro.org/living-abroad/how-many-americans-live-abroad); other estimates range 4.4M-8M
- **~720,000 US tax returns** filed from overseas addresses in 2021 (representing ~1.3M persons), per [AARO/IRS data](https://aaro.org/living-abroad/how-many-americans-live-abroad)
- **65 million Americans** aspire to become digital nomads (14M "definitely," 51M "maybe"), per [MBO Partners](https://www.mbopartners.com/state-of-independence/digital-nomads/)
- **69% of US nomads earn $50K-$250K/year** (34% earn $50-100K, 35% earn $100-250K), per [DemandSage](https://www.demandsage.com/digital-nomads-statistics/)
- **Only 4% of nomads spend the entire year abroad**; 39% travel exclusively within the US, per MBO Partners — meaning most face multi-state tax complexity rather than international-only issues
- **Expat tax prep costs $450-$530+** per return (Greenback $530 flat; Nomad Tax from $450 + add-ons for Schedule C, FEIE), with catch-up filing at $1,600 for 3 years
- **Domicile/mail forwarding services cost $150-$830/year** (Americas Mailbox $150-$230/yr; Earth Class Mail $828/yr; SavvyNomad bundles domicile + mail + CPA access)
- **TAM estimates by tool category**: Day tracker ~$90-185M/yr, Tax filing assistant ~$180-360M/yr, Domicile setup ~$45-90M/yr, FBAR monitor ~$18-36M/yr (see Full Findings for methodology)
- **83% of nomads are early tech adopters; 89% use AI** — high receptivity to software tools, per MBO Partners
- **IRS filing statistics for FBAR, Form 2555, Form 5471 are not publicly aggregated** in an easily accessible format; the 720K overseas-address returns is the best available proxy
- **The serviceable addressable market (SAM) narrows significantly**: only ~1.1M nomads travel internationally (4% full-year abroad + ~17% planning more international), and ~720K file from abroad

### 2. Americans Abroad & Tax Filing Data

Source: [AARO](https://aaro.org/living-abroad/how-many-americans-live-abroad)

Estimates of US citizens living abroad (non-military):
- AARO (2024): 5.5 million
- ACA/FVAP (2022): 4.4 million (FVAP); 5.1 million (ACA, including 1.2M gov't)
- World Population Review (2024): 8+ million
- UN (2020): ~3 million

Top countries: Mexico (1.18M), Canada (1.05M), UK (325K), Israel (281K), Germany (239K)

Tax filing from abroad: **719,690 US tax returns filed from overseas addresses** in calendar year 2021, representing approximately 1.3 million persons. This is the best available proxy for the number of Americans abroad who engage with IRS compliance.

### 3. IRS International Form Statistics

Despite multiple searches, specific aggregate filing counts for FBAR (FinCEN Form 114), Form 2555 (FEIE), and Form 5471 (foreign corporations) are not published in easily accessible public datasets. The IRS SOI division does not appear to publish a standalone count of these filings in a web-accessible format.

Best available proxies:
- **FBAR filers**: Likely in the range of 1-1.5M based on the ~720K overseas tax returns plus domestic filers with foreign accounts (anyone with >$10K in foreign accounts must file)
- **FEIE (Form 2555) claims**: A subset of the 720K overseas filers; most expats use FEIE, so likely 400-600K
- **Form 5471 filers**: Much smaller; applies to US persons with 10%+ ownership of foreign corporations. Likely in the tens of thousands (estimated 100-200K based on IRS enforcement context)

### 5. Price Sensitivity & Current Spending

**Tax preparation:**
- Greenback Expat Tax Services: $530 flat fee (federal), catch-up $1,600 for 3 years + 6 FBARs
- Nomad Tax (now defunct/redirected): was $450 base + $200 for FEIE, +$150 for Schedule C, +$50 per additional state
- H&R Block expat FBAR filing: available as add-on
- MyExpatTaxes (DIY software): ~$169-$249 per return
- General US tax prep average: $220-$350 (domestic); expat premium adds $200-$400

**Domicile setup:**
- SavvyNomad positions domicile setup at ~$1,200/year value (their estimate)
- Florida Declaration of Domicile: $0-$30 filing fee
- SD residency establishment: straightforward one-night stay + mail forwarding
- The real cost is ongoing maintenance: mail forwarding ($150-$830/yr), vehicle registration, voter registration

**Day tracking / compliance tools:**
- No dedicated paid tools found at scale; most nomads use spreadsheets or free apps
- This represents a green-field opportunity with low willingness-to-pay (likely $5-$15/mo)

### 6. TAM Calculations by Tool Category

**Methodology**: TAM = addressable population x estimated annual spend per user

#### Day Tracker (Physical Presence / Tax Day Counter)
- Addressable population: All 18.5M US nomads face some multi-state tracking need, but only those crossing state/country lines frequently need a tool. Conservative: ~2M active multi-state/international nomads
- Willingness to pay: $5-10/mo ($60-$120/yr) — utility app pricing
- **TAM: $120M-$240M/yr** (optimistic)
- **Realistic SAM**: ~1.5M users x $60-$120/yr = **$90M-$180M/yr**
- Near-term capturable: 50-100K early adopters x $60-$120/yr = **$3M-$12M/yr**

#### Tax Filing Assistant (Expat/Nomad-Specific)
- Addressable population: 720K overseas filers + estimated 500K-1M domestic nomads needing multi-state filing help = ~1.2-1.7M
- Current spend: $450-$530 for CPA; $169-$249 for DIY software
- Software price point: $99-$249/yr (below CPA, above TurboTax)
- **TAM: $180M-$360M/yr** (1.5M users x $120-$240/yr)
- Near-term capturable: 30-80K users x $150/yr = **$4.5M-$12M/yr**

#### Domicile Setup Service (Digital-First)
- Addressable population: New nomads establishing domicile each year. 73% of 18.5M have been nomadic <3 years, implying ~4.5M new entrants per year. Of those, perhaps 10-20% need domicile help = 450K-900K
- Price point: $200-$500 one-time + $100-$200/yr ongoing
- **TAM: $90M-$270M/yr** (600K x $150-$450)
- **Realistic SAM**: 150K actively seeking help x $300 = **$45M/yr**
- Near-term capturable: 10-20K users x $300 = **$3M-$6M/yr**

#### FBAR / Foreign Account Monitor
- Addressable population: Estimated 1-1.5M FBAR filers
- Willingness to pay: $15-$30/yr (simple compliance check) or bundled with tax prep
- **TAM: $15M-$45M/yr**
- **Realistic SAM**: 300K who would pay standalone x $60-$120/yr = **$18M-$36M/yr**
- Near-term capturable: 10-20K users x $60/yr = **$600K-$1.2M/yr**

#### Combined Near-Term Opportunity
A bundled micro-SaaS covering day tracking + tax guidance + domicile + FBAR at $15-$25/mo ($180-$300/yr) could target:
- Year 1-2: 5,000-20,000 users = **$900K-$6M ARR**
- Year 3-5: 50,000-100,000 users = **$9M-$30M ARR**

### 7. Key Uncertainties

1. The 18.5M figure includes many "domestic-only" nomads (39%) who may not need international tools
2. Willingness to pay for day-tracking is unproven — could be a feature, not a product
3. Tax prep is dominated by CPA relationships; software must overcome trust barrier
4. FBAR monitoring as standalone is likely too narrow; must bundle
5. IRS filing statistics are surprisingly hard to verify publicly
