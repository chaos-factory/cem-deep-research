## Full Findings

**Claim 1: 18.1M Americans identify as digital nomads in 2025, up 147% from 7.3M in 2019**
- Paper says: 18.1M Americans identify as digital nomads in 2025, up 147% from 7.3M in 2019, per MBO Partners 2025 Trends Report
- Found: The 18.1M figure is from MBO Partners' **August 2024** report (covering 2024 data), not the 2025 report. MBO Partners' 2025 report shows **18.5M** digital nomads. The 147% increase since 2019 is confirmed across multiple sources. The 7.3M 2019 baseline is mathematically consistent (18.1M / 2.47 = 7.33M) but was not independently stated in available sources. The paper appears to have conflated the 2024 data with the 2025 report label.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED
- Source: https://www.staffingindustry.com/news/global-daily-news/digital-nomads-move-from-niche-to-normal-with-181m-workers and https://www.facebook.com/mbopartners/photos/1451615482994137/

**Claim 2: 102.4% jump in Americans expatriating in Q1 2025 vs Q4 2024; projected 4,936 expatriations in 2025 (highest since 2020)**
- Paper says: 102.4% jump in Americans expatriating in Q1 2025 vs Q4 2024; projected 4,936 expatriations in 2025 (highest since 2020), per CS Global Partners
- Found: CS Global Partners confirms exactly: "there has been a 102.4% jump in Americans expatriating in the first quarter of 2025, compared to the last quarter of 2024." An estimated 1,285 Americans expatriated in Q1 2025. "Experts also predicted that by the end of 2025, the total number of Americans expatriating will reach 4,936... that would mark the highest annual total since 2020." All details match precisely.
- Verdict: CONFIRMED
- Source: https://csglobalpartners.com/news/us-expat-numbers-double-in-2025-as-americans-seek-new-lives-abroad/

**Claim 3: California FTB completed 520 residency audits in 2023, up from 230 in 2019 (126% increase). FTB penalty notices surged 32% in 2025.**
- Paper says: FTB completed 520 residency audits in 2023, up from 230 in 2019 (126% increase), per yourtaxbase.com. FTB penalty notices surged 32% in 2025, per corporatedirect.com.
- Found: The specific residency audit numbers (520 in 2023, 230 in 2019) could not be verified from any web source. The attributed source, yourtaxbase.com, is a Florida residency establishment service and does not publish FTB audit statistics. No other web source was found containing these specific numbers. The "FTB penalty notices surged 32% in 2025" claim is confirmed by ietaxattorney.com (a tax attorney site), though not by the attributed source corporatedirect.com. The 32% figure appears in a tax attorney's article but without a primary government source citation.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED (penalty surge confirmed from a secondary source; audit count numbers unverified)
- Source: https://ietaxattorney.com/california-ftb-audit-defense-how-state-tax-audits-differ-from-irs-audits/

**Claim 4: FEIE exclusion: $130,000 for 2025, $132,900 for 2026**
- Paper says: FEIE exclusion is $130,000 for 2025 and $132,900 for 2026, per IRS via multiple guides
- Found: Confirmed exactly. The IRS announced on October 9, 2025 that the FEIE for 2026 would be $132,900, up from $130,000 in 2025 (and $126,500 in 2024). Multiple authoritative tax sources confirm both figures.
- Verdict: CONFIRMED
- Source: https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/blog/irs-tax-inflation-adjustments-2026/ and https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/tax-saving-strategies/foreign-earned-income-exclusion.html
