## Full Findings

**Claim 1: 18.1M Americans identify as digital nomads in 2025, up 147% from 7.3M in 2019**
- Paper says: 18.1M Americans identify as digital nomads in 2025, up 147% from 7.3M in 2019, attributed to MBO Partners 2025 Trends Report
- Found: The 18.1M and 147% figures come from MBO Partners' **2024** State of Independence study, not the 2025 edition. The MBO Partners 2025 report (published late 2025) reports **18.5M** digital nomads and a **153%** increase since 2019. The 2024 data (18.1M/147%) was widely cited across media (US Chamber of Commerce, Staffing Industry Analysts, The Guardian, LinkedIn). The baseline of 7.3M in 2019 is consistent across all sources.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — the numbers are real but come from the 2024 report; the 2025 report shows updated, higher figures (18.5M / 153%).
- Source: https://www.mbopartners.com/state-of-independence/digital-nomads/ (2025 data); https://www.uschamber.com/co/grow/thrive/what-is-a-digital-nomad (2024 data citing 18.1M/147%)

**Claim 2: 102.4% jump in Americans expatriating in Q1 2025 vs Q4 2024; projected 4,936 expatriations in 2025 (highest since 2020)**
- Paper says: 102.4% jump in Americans expatriating in Q1 2025 vs Q4 2024; projected 4,936 expatriations in 2025 (highest since 2020), attributed to CS Global Partners
- Found: CS Global Partners' article confirms all details: "there has been a 102.4% jump in Americans expatriating in the first quarter of 2025, compared to the last quarter of 2024." The article states "an estimated 1,285 Americans left the country" in Q1 2025 and "by the end of 2025, the total number of Americans expatriating will reach 4,936" which "would mark the highest annual total since 2020." These figures are based on US Federal Register data. Corroborated by Miami Herald, WSJ (via Facebook), and FTAdviser.
- Verdict: CONFIRMED
- Source: https://csglobalpartners.com/news/us-expat-numbers-double-in-2025-as-americans-seek-new-lives-abroad/

**Claim 3: California FTB completed 520 residency audits in 2023, up from 230 in 2019 (126% increase). FTB penalty notices surged 32% in 2025.**
- Paper says: FTB completed 520 residency audits in 2023, up from 230 in 2019 (126% increase); FTB penalty notices surged 32% in 2025. Attributed to yourtaxbase.com, corporatedirect.com
- Found: The 520 vs 230 audit figures are confirmed. The SF Chronicle reports "The FTB completed about 520 residence and sourcing audits of individuals in the first 11 months of last year, compared with 230 in all of 2019." Corporate Direct confirms the same: "In 2023 alone, they completed 520 audits on out-of-state residents. This is up from the 230 that they completed in 2019." The 126% increase calculation is mathematically correct (520/230 - 1 = 1.26). However, the claim that "FTB penalty notices surged 32% in 2025" could not be verified from any web source, including yourtaxbase.com. Multiple searches returned no results matching this specific statistic.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — the 520/230 audit figures and 126% increase are confirmed; the "32% surge in penalty notices in 2025" is unconfirmed.
- Source: https://www.sfchronicle.com/california/article/state-tax-people-who-move-19416847.php ; https://www.corporatedirect.com/blog/does-california-have-an-exit-tax

**Claim 4: FEIE exclusion: $130,000 for 2025, $132,900 for 2026**
- Paper says: FEIE exclusion is $130,000 for 2025 and $132,900 for 2026, attributed to IRS via multiple guides
- Found: Fully confirmed. Greenback Tax Services states: "Foreign Earned Income Exclusion: $132,900 (up from $130,000 in 2025)." The IRS announced the 2026 figure via Revenue Procedure 2025-32 on October 9, 2025. Multiple tax advisory sites (TaxesForExpats, 1040Abroad, SmartAsset, OnlineTaxman) all confirm both figures.
- Verdict: CONFIRMED
- Source: https://www.greenbacktaxservices.com/blog/irs-tax-inflation-adjustments-2026/ ; https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-drop/rp-25-32.pdf
